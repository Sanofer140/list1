    1. Background

      2. Cricket's origins can be traced back to early medieval England, likely in the south-east with the earliest definite reference to the game appearing in the late 16th century. Initially a children's game, it gradually gained popularity among adults and evolved into the sport we know today.

    2. Foundation

      3. The foundation of cricket lies in 16th century rural England, where it began as a children's game before formalizing into a sport by the 18th century.

    3. Expansion and termination

      4. Cricket expanded globally through British colonial rule, spreading to countries like India, Australia, South Africa, and the Caribbean. While the sport thrived in many regions, it saw a decline or termination in some former colonies where other sports gained prominence.

  2. Administration

    3. The administration of cricket is governed globally by the International Cricket Council (ICC), which sets rules and organizes international tournaments. Each country has its own national governing body, like the BCCI in India or the ECB in England, managing domestic and international teams.

  3. Organisation

    4. The primary governing body for cricket in India is the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI). It is responsible for overseeing all cricket activities within India, including the national team and first-class cricket. The BCCI is a full member of the International Cricket Council (ICC)

    1. Tournament format

      2. Cricket tournaments are played in formats like round-robin, knockout, or a combination of both. Popular formats include Test series, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and T20 leagues like the IPL.

    2. Player acquisition,squad composition and salaries

      3. Players are acquired through auctions, drafts, or direct signings depending on the league or tournament. Squads are composed with a mix of domestic and international players, adhering to team size and player cap rules. Salaries vary based on player category, performance, and market demand, often governed by league-specific salary caps.

    3. Match rules

      4. Cricket matches are played between two teams of 11 players each, with one team batting and the other bowling/fielding.

    4. Prize money

      5. Prize money in cricket varies by tournament, with major events like the ICC World Cup and IPL offering multi-million-dollar rewards. It is distributed among winners, runners-up, and individual awardees for top performances.

  4. Teams

    5. Cricket teams represent countries in international matches (e.g., India, Australia, England) and franchises in leagues like the IPL or BBL. Each team consists of 11 players with roles such as batsmen, bowlers, all-rounders, and a wicketkeeper. Team selection is based on player form, fitness, and strategy for the match or tournament.
